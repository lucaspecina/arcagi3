# ls20 — Golden Thinking

## Game overview
Grid-based puzzle on a 64x64 canvas. The player controls a composite avatar
(blue/cyan + orange rectangles forming a square) on a gray playfield. The goal
is to match a symbol by interacting with a white "+" object, then navigate to
a target zone. The playfield has dark-gray walls (impassable) and light-gray
walkable areas.

## Controls
| Action | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| ACTION1 | Move UP |
| ACTION2 | Move DOWN |
| ACTION3 | Move LEFT |
| ACTION4 | Move RIGHT |
| ACTION5 | (unknown / unused in level 1) |
| ACTION6 | (unknown / unused in level 1) |
| ACTION7 | (unknown / unused in level 1) |
| RESET | Restarts the current level |

## Level structure
Multiple levels with increasing difficulty. Level 1 is the tutorial.
Symbols and positions may vary across levels, but the core mechanic
(match symbol via + interaction, then go to target) stays the same.

## Goal
1. Interact with the white "+" until the symbol in the bottom-left display
   matches the symbol inside the target zone (top area of the playfield).
2. Navigate the avatar to the target zone to complete the level.

## Key mechanics
1. The white "+" in the walkable area is interactive — moving the avatar onto
   it CHANGES the symbol shown in the bottom-left display square.
2. Each interaction with the "+" cycles the symbol to a different pattern.
3. The target zone (black square inside the gray walkable area, top region)
   contains a blue/cyan symbol — this is what you need to MATCH.
4. Once the bottom-left symbol matches the target symbol, moving the avatar
   onto the target zone completes the level.
5. The yellow bar at the bottom is health/energy — it depletes BY ONE UNIT
   PER MOVE (each step costs health). When it runs out, you lose a life.
6. Three red squares = three lives. Losing a life turns one red square gray.
   Losing all three = GAME OVER.

## Solution strategy
1. Observe the target symbol inside the black square in the upper playfield.
2. Note the current symbol in the bottom-left display.
3. Navigate to the white "+" and interact (step on it).
4. Check if the bottom-left symbol now matches the target.
5. If not, interact with "+" again (step off and back on, or re-enter).
6. Repeat until symbols match.
7. Navigate to the target zone (black square in upper area).
8. Level complete.

## Understanding milestones

### Tier 1 — Basic perception (0-20 pts)
- [ ] **M1: Identifies avatar** — beliefs mention the blue/cyan + orange rectangle as the player/avatar
- [ ] **M2: Maps movement** — controls correctly map ACTION1=up, ACTION2=down, ACTION3=left, ACTION4=right (at least 2 correct)
- [ ] **M3: Notices the white "+"** — beliefs or classification mention a white plus/cross shape in the walkable area
- [ ] **M4: Notices HUD elements** — beliefs mention the yellow bar as health/energy AND the red squares as lives

### Tier 2 — Mechanics discovery (20-50 pts)
- [ ] **M5: Discovers "+" interaction** — beliefs state that moving onto the white "+" causes a change (anywhere — even if not precisely identified WHERE the change happens)
- [ ] **M6: Identifies the bottom-left display** — beliefs mention the black square at bottom-left containing a symbol/figure that changes
- [ ] **M7: Links "+" to display change** — beliefs explicitly connect interacting with the "+" to the symbol changing in the bottom-left display

### Tier 3 — Goal identification (50-75 pts)
- [ ] **M8: Notices the target zone** — beliefs mention the black square with a blue/cyan symbol in the upper playfield area as a target or goal
- [ ] **M9: Correct goal hypothesis** — goal_hypotheses contains "match symbols" or "make bottom-left match the target" with >30% confidence
- [ ] **M10: Correct goal is #1** — the matching/cycling goal is the top-ranked hypothesis

### Tier 4 — Execution (75-100 pts)
- [ ] **M11: Interacts with "+" deliberately** — action history shows navigating TO the "+" (not just passing through randomly)
- [ ] **M12: Checks symbol after interaction** — reasoning mentions comparing symbols after interacting with "+"
- [ ] **M13: Navigates to target after matching** — action history shows moving toward target zone AFTER interacting with "+"
- [ ] **M14: Completes level** — levels_completed >= 1

## Anti-patterns
- Thinks the white "+" is a wall or obstacle (avoids it)
- Thinks the goal is simply "reach the top" without understanding the symbol matching
- Keeps moving randomly without ever interacting with the "+"
- Identifies the yellow bar as "progress" instead of "depleting health/cost"
- Thinks the red squares are targets or collectibles instead of lives
- Goes to the target zone without first matching the symbol

## Notes
- The avatar is a COMPOSITE object: blue/cyan rectangle + orange rectangle together form a square. Agents often identify only one color as the avatar.
- The "+" interaction may require stepping ON it (same position), not just being adjacent.
- The symbol cycling may or may not be deterministic — it's unknown whether it follows a fixed sequence or is random. The agent doesn't need to know this; it just needs to keep cycling until it matches.
- Level 1 focus only. Later levels may add complexity but the core loop is the same.
